About

A quick snapshot of who I am and what I teach.

Professional identity

I am a dedicated Uzbek language teacher with 1 year of experience. I’m actively reshaping my practice toward learner‑centred lessons where students collaborate, discuss, and solve problems while I guide and support.

Learners & context

I currently teach at an elementary private school. My learners are mostly grades 2–7, so I design tasks that are age‑appropriate, motivating, and supportive.

Interests & specialism

My professional interests include digital literacy in the classroom and Task‑Based Language Teaching (TBLT)—using real communication and purposeful tasks to build fluency and confidence.

Teaching with purpose

I believe language learning is a social process. When students interact with meaningful content and each other, they build language plus key life skills—collaboration, critical thinking, and confidence.

  • Student‑centred classroom: learners talk, discuss, solve problems
  • TBLT: tasks first, rules discovered through use
  • Delayed feedback: fluency first, reflection later
  • Scaffolding: visuals and sentence starters for inclusivity
